Common Issues Among Students

Students commonly face stress, anxiety, depression, burnout, overthinking, loneliness, sleep problems, and low confidence. Early awareness helps intervene.

Stress & pressure
Anxiety & worry
Depression & low mood
Loneliness & isolation

Short list

  • Stress (exams, deadlines)
  • Anxiety (worry & panic)
  • Depression (persistent low mood)
  • Burnout & overwork
  • Sleep problems & fatigue
  • Low self-esteem & comparison

Warning Signs

  • Persistent sadness & mood swings
  • Withdrawal from friends and activities
  • Loss of interest in hobbies or studies
  • Sleep disturbances (too much or too little)
  • Chronic fatigue & low energy
  • Hopelessness & difficulty concentrating
Recognize the signs early

How to Take Care of Your Mental Health

  • Sleep: 7‚Äì9 hours when possible
  • Nutrition: Balanced meals & hydration
  • Exercise: Move daily to boost mood
  • Mindfulness: Breathing, meditation, journaling
  • Boundaries: Limit screen time & say no
  • Support: Talk to friends, family, counsellors
Self-care matters

How to Help a Friend

  • Listen without judgment
  • Validate their feelings & be patient
  • Offer practical help and check-in
  • Encourage professional help when needed
  • If immediate risk, contact emergency services
Supportive presence

Myths vs Facts

Talking about it makes it worseTalking often relieves burden and helps recovery.
Therapy is only for severe casesTherapy helps with a wide range of problems and is proactive.
